Changing sustainable behaviour

Our current goal is focused on changing the behaviour of the guest in a sustainable way. In the overview below, our ideas are briefly explained. We will discuss them more in-depth later on.

Prototype 1: MIA - Motivation Intelligent Assistant

MIA is voice assistance that guests can access through mirrors and screens around the hotel.

It helps guests to set a 'sustainability goal' to achieve during their stay, and suggests making conscious choices in the hotel, such as using stairs instead of elevators or shortening the shower time.

During their stay, the achievement is visualized on the mirror to make guests to be motivated for the behaviors.

In the prototype, we show the conversation setting the ‘sustainable challenge’. It consists of asking each other names, the intention to the challenge, choosing the challenge type and difficulty.

Prototype 2: Reducing water use

In this prototype we focussed on the usage of water during their stay. We did not want to signal off any indication that we disapprove extra long showers, but make them of how long they are showering, by slowly fading the lights. The lights will turn on after the edge impulse has detected the shower sound.

In the first prototype MIA has proposed a few challenges, one of those challenges is having a sustainable shower duration, 3, 5 or 7 minutes. When the guest has chosen 5 minutes, the lights will be out after 5 minutes. When the shower has been turned off in time, the shower can give positive feedback.

Prototype 3: Stimulating stair usage

MIA will recognize if guests are arriving or are already checked in, due to recognizing of luggage. When it recognizes that the guest doesn’t have any luggage with them, and he/she is waiting for the elevator, it will start talking to the guest and stimulate him/her to use the stairs instead. We want to make the stair-use a interactive and fun experience.
