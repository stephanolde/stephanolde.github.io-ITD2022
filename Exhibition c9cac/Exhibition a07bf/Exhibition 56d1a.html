Prototype 1: Skiing Posture Recognition ‚õ∑Ô∏è

Idea:

The idea of the prototype is to use posture recognition to recognise skiing posture. This could be used by beginner skiers to improve their posture as they are often not aware of their own posture while skiing. The concept would use external cameras to film skiers going down the slopes. Beginner posture is classified as passive and backward leaning while the experts actively engage their body and lean forward.

Prototype:

While training the prototype we used three people for picture input in front of a neutral white background. We filmed from the side with the participants facing the same direction. The postures are exaggerated.

Testing:

The prototype was tested at home due to storm Eunice in a different background with different participants that have no skiing experience. They were asked to use the AI to improve their posture.

Video:

Insights:

  • The AI recognised the different postures
  • The AI has difficulty recognising other people than the input footage
  • The AI has difficulty recognising postures with a busy background
  • The AI was very sensitive and reactive
  • The participants didn‚Äôt know how to improve their posture

Implications:

  • The AI should include different types of postures.
  • The AI should differentiate the different elements of the posture: back lean, knee bend, arm angle, etc.
  • The AI should give feedback which elements of the posture should be improved and how.
  • The posture analysis could be communicated in AR through the skiing helmet.

Prototype 2: Voice Controlled Skiing Lessons üëÇ

Idea:

This prototype lets the user take voice controlled skiing lessons without the use of an instructor. It works by using audio recognition AI embedded in the skiing helmet. The user has a dialogue with their helmet which suggests different challenging or relaxing lessons. The system will reward the user with points and level-ups for completing lessons.

Prototype:

The menu is designed to be as intuitive as possible. The user can choose between relaxing or challenging which both offer a set of lessons.

Testing:

The original test setup was standing in front of a POV skiing video without seeing the AI using wireless headphones to communicate. Due to some technical difficulties this was downscaled to talking to the AI with the video next to the screen. The prototype was tested with three participants.

Insights:

  • The AI had difficulty with recognising other accents or names.
  • The AI only recognised words when speaking very clearly and deliberately.
  • The conversation style and timing made people have to actively think and remember options.
  • Errors in the conversation made the flow end
  • The conversation was distracting while skiing, in the test there were already too many variables for the participants to answer the questions.
  • Faulty AI can trigger emotions and frustrate people

Implications:

  • The conversation style should be less distracting and mentally demanding.
  • The timing should be slowed down.
