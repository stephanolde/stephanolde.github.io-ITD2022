The brief

To improve the preoperative experience of oncological patients who will undergo surgical reconstruction of either face or breast. This experience has a huge psychological impact on both the patient and the companion. Research shows that support from people with the same experiences will make you feel less alone in this situation. [SOURCE]

For all our different concepts we are focussing on the mental wellbeing of the patient and/or companion.

1. Reassurance

2. Community

3. Sharing

4. Self-perception

Aspect 1

Reassurance

Sometimes a gesture, an advice, an opinion, an action or just information can make people feel comforted and regain confidence. But for these stimuli to be effective, they need to be considerate of the person’s emotional and mental state.

The emotional journey of a patient can be veering and unpredictable, so we would like them to learn about their procedure at the right time and at their own pace.

Facial and posture recognition through machine learning allows for emotion identification, which might help inform the patient or direct the aforementioned stimuli in a timely manner.

First prototypes and explorations

The focus was to explore emotion recognition so the friend/buddy can respond more accurately to the patient’s emotional status.

Aspect 2

Community

Going through and illness can become a lonely process since it is hard to assimilate and convey thoughts and emotions. Knowing that there is people with unique struggle stories as well can help patients see they are not alone.

Aspect 3

Sharing

A device that can guide you in formulating a story and/or message you want to share with others to help them in their process of breast reconstruction surgery. In this concept, we reserve the previous concept — CommuniTree and focus on building a device to record some patient stories and share them. Other patients come to the device and speak to the device, the device will give some light effects and start to play the recording of the last patients’. The current patient can also record his/ her stories to the next patient. This device gives people a positive impact and supports people before or after surgery.
