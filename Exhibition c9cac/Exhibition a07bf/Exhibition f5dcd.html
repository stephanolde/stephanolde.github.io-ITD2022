We are Flora, Rahul, Kang and Benthe, and together we form Team 22. In the past few weeks, we have spent every Friday creating prototypes to help the Erasmus MC improving their patient experience. We experimented with three prototypes, targeting different phases and issues during the process leading up to the surgery.

THE CONTEXT

The Erasmus MC

GOAL

Design an interactive product or environment that enables optimization of the preoperative experience for patients opting for oncological facial and breast reconstruction.

OUR THREE PROTOTYPES: SOLUTION SPACES COVERED

Over the past three weeks, we created a new prototype every week. We tried to cover a different aspect of the pre-operative experience with every prototype, looking at different phases in the user journey.

Apart from covering different solution spaces, we also tried out different technologies. The technologies that we used are:

  • Teachable Machine: use AI for image recognition
  • AI conversation software
  • AR-software
  • Arduino to create a physical interface

PROTOTYPE 1 - ‘BEAUTY IDEAL OR NOT’

Uncovering unreachable beauty standards portrayed by the media for women who prepare to undergo reconstructive surgery.

  • Making patients aware of the media’s influence on perceived beauty standards
  • Decrease the negative impact of social media on self-image and self-confidence
  • Comparing ratio - ‘beauty standard’ faces and ‘normal - mundane’ faces

Using AI (Teachable Machine) to recognise unrealistic/ edited/filtered photographs and mark them on social media.

INSIGHTS:

  • People were puzzled about what to do with the labels that they were presented with
  • Emotional responses to the labels included: flattery, witty, but also confusion and feeling offended.

PROTOTYPE 2 - ‘MY AI FRIEND’

Providing a supportive ‘friend’ to share worries, feelings about their upcoming surgery and getting more information.

  • A chatbot for patients to talk to
  • Safe space for them to share their thoughts, and be understood

We created 3 personas to chat with in the form of former patients. (Depending what the patient needs - supportive Josh, informative Emma and affirmative Wouter)

  • Different tone of voices and personality
  • Making it personal and informal

INSIGHTS:

  • there were emotions, very two-fold being pulled in and pushed, weird but intriguing
